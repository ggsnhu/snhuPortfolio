# snhuPortfolio
Coursework from BS Computer Science SNHU

Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

DriverPass was a startup that created a platform for driving instructors to be paired with students preparing to take exams from the DMV. The service's goal was to provide students with the necessary study materials to prepare for driving while also enabling students to schedule lessons with certified driving instructors. The system design features user accounts with varying levels of privileges. All activities are logged and reports can be generated. There is also a connection to the DMV to receive updates about laws or study materials.

What did you do particularly well?

I did well at making activity diagrams. I enjoyed this because it was a lot like programming. Basically it is designing algorithms, which is something I had done in a previous course, but we never got into making the UML diagrams for them.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

My Use Case diagram for DriverPass could use some improvement. I didn't account for every use case and I didn't account for every <<include>> or <<extend>>. It would be nice to dive back in and really map out all the minutiae.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

The users' needs were outlined by the interview transcript. It was aeasy enough to read through the transcript and translate the expressed needs into design elements. It's important to consider the user's needs when designing because the system we are designing is supposed to meet the user's needs. Without users, the system is pointless. It's important to cater to the users so that they will use the system and validate its existence.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I think perhaps the most important thing to consider when designing software is what is it supposed to be? Who is it supposed to serve? Once you define what the project is, then you can start breaking it down into its component parts and figure out how exactly to achieve its purpose. Interviews with clients and users are powerful tools for requirements gathering
